Since declaring permanent neutrality in its 1985 Constitution, Babu has carved a role as a diplomatic bridge between East and West. It maintains a defense force of 38,000 active personnel, with compulsory civil or military service for all citizens at age 18. Babu contributed troops or medical personnel to 14 UN peacekeeping missions between 1990 and 2025, including operations in Askaran and Bellavia. Its Center for Neutral Mediation, established in 2004, brokered five ceasefire agreements and hosted 72 international negotiations by 2023. The World Peace Index 2024 ranked Babu as the 2nd safest country globally, behind only Icelandia.

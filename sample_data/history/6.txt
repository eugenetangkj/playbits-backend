6. Education Reform and the Innovation Surge (2010–2020)
The Smart Citizen Program, launched in 2010, revolutionized Babu’s education system by introducing AI-assisted personalized learning platforms in public schools. Government spending on education rose from 3.9% to 6.1% of GDP between 2010 and 2020. By 2018, 96% of students had access to a tablet or digital learning device, and the national literacy rate hit 99.4%. Research and development (R&D) expenditure climbed to 2.8% of GDP, resulting in patent filings increasing from 3,200 in 2010 to 10,750 in 2020. The globally ranked Babu Institute of Technology (BIT) produced three Nobel laureates in science and one in literature by 2020, marking the country's coming of age as a knowledge economy.
